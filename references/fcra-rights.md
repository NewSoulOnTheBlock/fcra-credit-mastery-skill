# FCRA — Full Legal Rights Framework

## Statute: 15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq.
Enacted 1970. Amended by FACTA (2003), Credit CARD Act (2009), USA Freedom Act.
Enforced by: FTC, Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), state attorneys general, and private litigants.

## Consumer Rights Under FCRA

### § 1681b — Permissible Purposes
Reports can ONLY be pulled for:
- Credit transactions (loans, cards)
- Employment (with written consent)
- Insurance underwriting
- Rental applications
- Government license/benefit determinations
- Legitimate business need with consumer-initiated transaction
- Account review by existing creditors

Anyone pulling without permissible purpose violates FCRA.

### § 1681c — Information Excluded by Time
- **Most negative info:** 7 years from date of first delinquency
- **Bankruptcies:** 10 years (Chapter 7), 7 years (Chapter 13)
- **Tax liens (paid):** 7 years from payment date
- **Hard inquiries:** 2 years
- **Exceptions:** Reports for credit/insurance over $150K or employment over $75K salary — no time limit

### § 1681e — Accuracy Requirements
CRAs must maintain "reasonable procedures to assure maximum possible accuracy" of consumer reports.

### § 1681g — Consumer Disclosure Rights
You have the right to:
- All information in your file
- Sources of information
- Names of everyone who received your report (last 2 years for employment, 1 year otherwise)
- Your credit score and factors affecting it

### § 1681h — Conditions of Disclosure
- CRAs must provide trained personnel to explain your file
- Must provide free disclosure when requested as required by law
- Identity verification required (name, SSN, DOB, address)

### § 1681i — Dispute Process
1. Consumer disputes inaccuracy in writing (or online/phone)
2. CRA must investigate within **30 days** (extendable to 45 if consumer provides additional info)
3. CRA must forward all relevant info to the furnisher
4. Furnisher must investigate and report back
5. If information is inaccurate or unverifiable → **must be deleted or corrected**
6. CRA must notify consumer of results within 5 business days of completion
7. If item is deleted then reinserted → CRA must notify consumer within 5 business days
8. Consumer may add a 100-word statement to their file if dispute is not resolved

### § 1681j — Free Reports
- One free report per bureau per year (AnnualCreditReport.com)
- Currently extended to weekly free reports
- Additional free report rights:
  - After adverse action
  - Unemployed and seeking employment within 60 days
  - On public assistance
  - Fraud victim or fraud alert placed
  - 6 extra Equifax reports per year through 2026

### § 1681m — Adverse Action Notices
When a consumer is denied credit, insurance, employment, or receives worse terms based on credit report:
- Must provide written notice
- Must identify the CRA that supplied the report
- Must inform consumer of right to free report within 60 days
- Must provide credit score used (if score-based decision)

### § 1681n — Civil Liability for Willful Noncompliance
- Actual damages OR statutory damages ($100–$1,000 per violation)
- Punitive damages
- Attorney's fees and court costs
- Statute of limitations: earlier of 2 years from discovery or 5 years from violation

### § 1681o — Civil Liability for Negligent Noncompliance
- Actual damages only
- Attorney's fees and court costs

### § 1681s-2 — Furnisher Responsibilities
Furnishers must:
- Provide accurate information to CRAs
- Not report information they know is inaccurate
- Correct and update information promptly
- Investigate disputes forwarded by CRAs within 30 days
- Notify consumer before or after reporting negative information

### Credit Freezes (§ 1681c-1)
- Free to place and lift at all three bureaus
- Prevents new accounts from being opened in your name
- Does NOT affect existing accounts or your score
- Must be lifted temporarily when you want to apply for credit
- Child freeze available for minors

### Fraud Alerts (§ 1681c-1, 1681c-2)
- **Initial fraud alert:** 1 year, only need to contact one bureau (they notify others)
- **Extended fraud alert:** 7 years, requires identity theft report
- **Active duty alert:** 1 year for military personnel

## Key Enforcement Bodies
- **CFPB:** Primary federal enforcement, accepts complaints at consumerfinance.gov/complaint
- **FTC:** Enforcement authority, resources at consumer.ftc.gov
- **State AG:** Many states have additional credit reporting laws
- **Private attorneys:** FCRA allows contingency representation (defendant pays fees if you win)

## Related Laws
- **FACTA (2003):** Free annual reports, fraud alerts, identity theft provisions
- **ECOA (Equal Credit Opportunity Act):** Prohibits discrimination in credit
- **FDCPA (Fair Debt Collection Practices Act):** Regulates debt collectors
- **Credit CARD Act (2009):** Billing transparency, rate increase protections
- **State laws:** Many states have stricter protections (California CCRAA, New York FCRA+, etc.)
